Catholic Teaching on Suicide & Mental Health

The Catholic Church recognizes that suicide is a grave matter, but emphasizes God's infinite mercy and understanding of mental health issues. The Church teaches that psychological disturbances, anguish, grave fear of hardship, suffering, or torture can diminish the responsibility of the person who dies by suicide.

"We should not despair of the eternal salvation of persons who have taken their own lives. By ways known to him alone, God can provide the opportunity for salutary repentance." - Catechism of the Catholic Church, 2283

Our ministry approaches mental health and suicide prevention with compassion, recognizing that those who struggle deserve our prayers, support, and accompaniment on their journey toward healing.

Our Programs & Services

QPR Training

Question. Persuade. Refer.

Learn the 3 simple steps anyone can use to help save a life from suicide. Our training programs equip community members to recognize warning signs and intervene effectively.

Support Groups

Connect with others who understand the challenges of mental illness or the loss of a loved one to suicide. Find community, hope, and healing in a faith-filled environment.

Grief Ministry

Compassionate support for those who have lost a loved one to suicide. We offer specialized resources and accompaniment through the journey of grief and healing.

Education & Awareness

Workshops, seminars, and resources to break the stigma around mental illness and promote understanding within the Catholic community.
