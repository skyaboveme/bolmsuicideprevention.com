Written by Catholics who have lost a loved one to suicide, this book offers personal stories of loss, coping with crushing grief, finding comfort in faith and community, and discovering hope.

Topics include:

  • Personal narratives of loss and healing
  • Catholic perspectives on grief and hope
  • Practical guidance for surviving family members
  • Finding support in faith communities

Responding to Suicide: A Pastoral Handbook

The first book written for Catholic leaders that takes a holistic approach to understanding suicide and ministering effectively in its aftermath.

Topics include:

  • Understanding suicide from medical, psychological, and spiritual perspectives
  • Pastoral care for those affected by suicide
  • Liturgical considerations and funeral planning
  • Creating supportive parish communities

Training & Education Programs

QPR (Question, Persuade, Refer) Training

Learn the three simple steps anyone can take to help save a life from suicide:

  • Question: Ask directly about suicidal thoughts
  • Persuade: Encourage the person to seek help
  • Refer: Connect them with appropriate resources

This evidence-based program equips community members to recognize warning signs and intervene effectively.

Prayers for Mental Health & Healing

Prayer for Those Struggling with Mental Illness

Loving God, you are the source of all hope and healing. Be with those who struggle with mental illness. Grant them comfort in their pain, strength in their weakness, and hope in their despair. May they know that they are precious in your sight and never alone. Through Christ our Lord. Amen.

Prayer for Those Who Have Lost a Loved One to Suicide

Merciful Father, we entrust to your infinite love our beloved [name], who has died by suicide. In your boundless mercy, grant them eternal rest and peace. Console all who grieve this profound loss, and help us to trust in your compassion and understanding. May we find comfort in your promise of eternal life. Amen.

Recognizing Warning Signs

Talk

If a person talks about:

  • Killing themselves
  • Feeling hopeless
  • Having no reason to live
  • Being a burden to others
  • Feeling trapped
  • Unbearable pain

Behavior

Behaviors that may signal risk:

  • Increased use of alcohol or drugs
  • Looking for ways to end their life
  • Withdrawing from activities
  • Isolating from family and friends
  • Sleeping too much or too little
  • Visiting or calling to say goodbye
  • Giving away prized possessions
  • Aggression or fatigue

Mood

People who are considering suicide often display:

  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • Loss of interest
  • Irritability
  • Humiliation
  • Agitation or rage
  • Relief or sudden improvement (after a period of being down)

What to Do

If you notice these warning signs in someone you know:

  1. Ask: "Are you thinking about suicide?" (Asking will not put the idea in their head)
  2. Listen: Without judgment, let them share what they're feeling
  3. Stay: Don't leave them alone if you believe they're in immediate danger
  4. Connect: Help them reach out to crisis support (988) or other resources
  5. Follow up: Check in, show you care, and continue to be supportive
